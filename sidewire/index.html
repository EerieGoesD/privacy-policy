# SideWire Privacy Policy Last updated: June 14, 2026 SideWire is an app for sending messages and files between your own devices. It runs on Windows, macOS, Android, and iOS. This policy describes what data SideWire accesses, stores, and processes, for all components published by EERIE under the SideWire name. ## Summary SideWire has two ways to connect, and they handle data differently: - **Local mode** keeps everything on your devices and your local Wi-Fi network. Nothing is sent to any server operated by us. - **Remote mode** relays your room through a server we operate, but your messages, files, and file names are **end-to-end encrypted on your device** before they are sent. The relay only ever sees ciphertext and cannot read your content. Your room password is never sent to the relay. SideWire has no user accounts, no analytics or telemetry, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. ## Local mode When you create a Local room, the app starts a small server on your device and announces the room on your local network so other devices on the same Wi-Fi can find it. When you join a Local room, your device talks directly to the host device over the local network. - Messages and files are exchanged directly between devices on your network. - Files received by a host are stored, encrypted, in a temporary folder on the host device and are automatically deleted after about an hour. - No data leaves your local network, and none of it reaches any server operated by us. A Local host also serves a simple web page so a phone's browser can connect without installing the app. This page communicates only with the host on your local network. ## Remote mode When you use Remote mode, your room is relayed through a server we operate (hosted on Fly.io). Remote rooms require a password. **What is end-to-end encrypted (we cannot read it):** - Message text - File contents - File names These are encrypted on your device using a key derived from your room password (PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256). The relay only forwards the resulting ciphertext. Your password is never transmitted; joining is gated by a separate one-way hash so the relay can admit members without learning the password or the encryption key. **What the relay necessarily processes to operate the service:** - Your IP address (seen by the server for any internet connection) - The room code - The device display name you choose - Connection timestamps - The size and count of messages and files (for abuse prevention and rate limiting) The relay cannot decrypt your messages, files, or file names. ## What is stored - **On your device:** your chosen device name, theme preference, the current room code, and conversations you explicitly save (kept in your app data folder until you delete them). These never leave your device unless you are in a room and send them. - **On the relay (Remote mode only):** rooms are held in memory only and are lost when the room closes or the server restarts; they are not written to a database. Operational logs (which include IP addresses, room codes, device display names, and message/file sizes - never content) are kept for abuse prevention. The hosting platform (Fly.io) retains platform logs for a limited period (about 7 days). ## Administrative access The relay exposes diagnostic endpoints used only by the operator for abuse monitoring. These require a secret key and are otherwise disabled; they are not publicly accessible. ## What is NOT collected SideWire does NOT collect, transmit, or store any of the following: - Analytics or usage statistics - Crash reports or telemetry - Advertising identifiers - Email addresses, account identifiers, or contact lists - The contents of your messages or files (these are end-to-end encrypted in Remote mode and never leave your network in Local mode) SideWire contains no third-party trackers, advertising SDKs, or analytics libraries. ## Third-party services The only third-party service involved is the hosting provider for the Remote relay (Fly.io), which processes the connection metadata described above in order to run the server. The relay is open source and can be self-hosted if you prefer to run your own. ## Children's privacy SideWire is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect any data from anyone. ## Security Remote content is end-to-end encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a key derived from your room password; the relay never receives the password or the key. Local mode keeps traffic on your own network. As with any password-based system, choose a strong room password and share it only with people you trust. ## Your choices - Use Local mode to keep everything on your own network. - Leave a room at any time; for Remote rooms this closes your connection to the relay. - Delete saved conversations at any time from within the app. - Uninstall the app at any time through your platform's standard uninstall flow. ## Changes to this policy If this policy is updated, the new version will be posted at this URL and the "Last updated" date will reflect the most recent revision. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes. ## Contact For questions about this privacy policy, open an issue at https://github.com/EerieGoesD/sidewire/issues or email eeriegoesd@gmail.com.
